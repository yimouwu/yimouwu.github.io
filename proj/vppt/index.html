Abstract

TL;DR VPPT bridges feed-forward 3D foundation models with the imperfect pose priors ubiquitous on real robotic platforms. A residual identity shortcut (Prior Connection) carries the sensor prior around the visual backbone, so the model stays stable where both prior-free reconstructors and prior-dominated filters diverge.

Robust 3D reconstruction and camera tracking from real-world streaming monocular video remain a central bottleneck for embodied intelligence, requiring simultaneous real-time efficiency, temporal stability, and metric-scale accuracy. Vision-only feed-forward pipelines often degrade in visually challenging environments—textureless corridors, specular endoscopic views, low-light or foggy outdoor scenes—while classical visual-inertial systems depend on handcrafted filtering assumptions that become brittle when sensor priors are partial, noisy, or unreliable.

We present the Vision-Pose-Prior Transformer (VPPT), a feed-forward streaming model that jointly recovers metric 3D pointmaps and camera trajectories from a monocular stream paired with a real-world, sensor-level pose prior. To balance the sensor prior and geometry learning, VPPT introduces Deep Prior Learning through a dual-path prior injection mechanism, where the pose prior simultaneously serves as a geometric conditioning signal for visual correspondence reasoning and as an identity mapping for stable temporal pose refinement. By coupling learned visual geometry with a Prior Connection pathway, VPPT remains stable under visual degradation and noisy pose priors. Extensive experiments demonstrate that VPPT remains stable where both prior-free reconstructors and prior-dominated filters diverge, bridging feed-forward 3D foundation models with the imperfect priors ubiquitous on real robotic platforms.

  • 01 Streaming joint vision-prior 3D foundation model. A feed-forward 3D foundation model that ingests a real, imperfect pose prior as a first-class input, jointly recovering metric pointmaps and a camera trajectory from a monocular stream.
  • 02 Prior Connection. A residual identity shortcut that routes the prior around the visual backbone to the pose head; a recurrent pose decoder then learns a reliability-aware fusion of the prior and the visual token.
  • 03 Evaluation across domains and noise levels. Evaluated on degraded, driving, endoscopic, and indoor dynamic environments, together with held-out domains and a five-level prior-noise sweep. VPPT improves trajectory and depth accuracy and degrades gracefully as prior quality drops.

Method

VPPT extends the DUSt3R family of pairwise feed-forward 3D regressors to streaming, prior-conditioned reconstruction. At its core is Deep Prior Learning: when a pose prior is injected only as a decoder conditioning signal, it must survive the visual backbone; under degraded vision the attention carrying the prior is dominated by unreliable visual content, so the prior is progressively diluted—a failure mode we call prior dilution.

The Prior Connection solves this by carrying the prior through a parameter-free identity shortcut that bypasses the visual backbone entirely. A lightweight LSTM temporal decoder receives both the visual pose token (which may be degraded) and the untouched prior embedding, and learns a reliability-aware correction: small when the prior is informative, and yielding to vision when it is not. A dedicated Scale token recovers metric scale from the prior rather than from range sensing.

Network Architecture. A shared ViT-L encoder extracts patch tokens; dual decoders with Pose and Scale special tokens communicate via cross-attention; the Prior Connection (identity shortcut) routes the pose prior directly to the LSTM pose decoder, bypassing the visual backbone.
Deep Prior Learning: a building block. Dual-pathway fusion of a sensor pose prior with a visual pose estimate via an identity shortcut and a lightweight temporal decoder.

VPPT is evaluated on held-out benchmarks spanning outdoor driving (KITTI), endoscopic (EndoSLAM), indoor dynamic (TUM/Bonn), and extreme degradation (SubT-MRS) domains. Metrics include ATE, RPE (translation and rotation), and depth accuracy (Abs-Rel, RMSE, δ<1.25).
